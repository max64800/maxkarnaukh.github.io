How It Works

A System Built in Eight Parts.

Each part depends on the one before it. Most outbound failures trace back to skipping the first two.

Step 01
ICP Definition
Who you are selling to. Who you are ignoring. What problem they have that your offer solves. Most outbound fails here, before a single message is sent. If this is not defined precisely, everything downstream is a guess.
Foundation
Step 02
Lead Targeting
Where to find the right companies and contacts. Not volume. Precision. The goal is a targeted list of people who match the ICP, not the largest list possible.
Targeting
Step 03
Data and Enrichment
Decision-makers, not gatekeepers. Context about their business, not just a name and a number. Knowing something specific about who you are calling changes the entire quality of the conversation.
Intelligence
Step 04
Outreach System
Cold calls, email, and LinkedIn working together as one coordinated sequence. Each channel reinforces the others. The message is consistent. The timing is deliberate.
Execution
Step 05
Qualification System
Fast disqualification. Identify real opportunities quickly and stop spending time on people who are not a fit. A tight qualification framework is what separates a high-converting pipeline from a busy one.
Filtering
Step 06
Follow-Up Engine
Most deals close on follow-up five or six. Most reps stop at two. This builds the structured sequence that ensures nothing is dropped. This is where most revenue actually comes from.
Revenue Recovery
Step 07
Sales Execution
Discovery calls. Objection handling. Closing. Built around your offer and your buyer. Something your team can use tomorrow on a real call and know exactly what to do.
Conversion
Step 08
Team Training
Making this repeatable without you in the room. Live roleplay. Call reviews. Drilling real scenarios until the responses become automatic. The system only works if the people running it know how to run it.

In Practice

Precision Over Volume.

Real Estate Custom Software

A real estate client had a pipeline of over 35,000 agents on Property Finder. The team had volume. What they did not have was any way to know who was actually worth calling.

We built custom software that investigated every agent on the platform. It analyzed listing activity, response patterns, deal volume, and behavioral signals to score each agent and determine their probability of converting.

The system filtered 35,000 agents down to approximately 3,000 high-probability targets. The team stopped burning time on low-intent contacts and started every call knowing that person was likely to convert.

Call quality went up. Meetings went up. Wasted time went down significantly. This is what proper ICP and targeting work looks like. Not more outreach. Better outreach.

Common Questions

Straight Answers.

If you have a question not listed here, ask it on the call.

Is this just lead generation?
No. Leads are not the problem for most companies. They already have access to more potential targets than they can handle.

The problem is targeting the right ones, having a system to reach them properly, and a team that can qualify and close when people respond.

Lead generation is one component. The system is what I deliver.
Do you guarantee results?
No. Anyone guaranteeing results without controlling your offer, your market, and your execution is guessing.

What I build is the system that gives you the highest probability of consistent outcomes. The results depend on both sides executing.
We already have a sales team. Does this still apply?
This works best when there is already a team in place. The focus is improving how they operate, not replacing them.

A good team running a broken system produces weak results. The same team running a proper system produces consistently better ones.
We tried outbound before and it did not work.
Most outbound fails for the same reasons:
  • ICP was never properly defined
  • Targeting was based on volume, not fit
  • No real qualification framework existed
  • No follow-up system past the second attempt
If those are not fixed, outbound will fail regardless of who is running it. This process starts by addressing all four.
Is this for any industry?
Not every industry, but it works for most B2B companies that have:
  • A clear, defined offer
  • Deal value that justifies the cost of outbound
  • Willingness to execute the system once it is built
If those conditions are not in place, I will say so on the first call.
Do you provide leads or tools?
Yes. I build a custom outbound system for your business and provide the tools to optimize, fix, and scale it.

The system covers targeting, outreach, qualification, and follow-up. The tools are built specifically around your market and your offer. Nothing is off-the-shelf.
How long until we see results?
Depends on your current setup. Some changes improve performance within a couple of weeks. A fully built system typically takes several weeks to implement and refine.

I will give you a specific answer based on your situation on the first call.
Do I need to be involved?
Yes, at the start. You know your product, your market, and your clients better than anyone. That knowledge is required to build the system correctly.

The goal is a system that eventually runs without constant founder involvement. But that is the outcome, not the starting point.
Is this right for early-stage companies?
Usually not. You need a proven offer and some level of product-market fit before outbound makes sense.

If you are still figuring out who your customer is or whether your product works, outbound is not the priority.
What happens on the first call?
We look at your current pipeline, your target market, and where deals are breaking down.

If there is a clear fit, I will tell you exactly what I would build and what to expect. If there is not a fit, I will tell you that too. No pitch. Just an honest conversation.
