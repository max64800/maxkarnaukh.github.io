Here Is How I Think

This is the part that either resonates with you completely, or tells you I am not the right fit. Either way, you will know in 60 seconds.

01

Only Sell to People Who Genuinely Need It

Qualifying out fast is a sign of respect. Closing a prospect who has no real need for your product or service is the worst thing you can do. They will hate the experience, leave a bad review, and never refer anyone. A fast "no" beats a slow bad "yes" every time.

02

Provide Real Value Before Asking for Anything

The sale is a natural result of that. When a prospect feels like the conversation already helped them, closing becomes a formality.

03

Cold Calling Is Not Dead

It is at its peak effectiveness right now, because no one does it anymore. Every founder is hiding behind email and LinkedIn. The person who actually picks up the phone wins by default.

04

Sales Is Education, Not Performance

You should never be trying to "sell" anyone. You should be showing them, clearly and specifically, why your product or service fixes the exact problem they are sitting with right now. If you are reciting a script, you are performing. If you understand their problem better than they do, you are selling.

05

Systems Beat Individual Talent. Every Time.

A struggling salesperson with a great system will run laps around a naturally gifted closer with no process. Stop trying to hire your way out of a systems problem. Build the infrastructure around your people, and watch average performers become consistent ones.
